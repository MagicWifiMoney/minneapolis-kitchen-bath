# YouTube + Pinterest Content Plan

Minneapolis Kitchen & Bath. Long-tail visual SEO play. Built to compound over 12 to 18 months on YouTube and 3 to 6 months on Pinterest.

Realistic timelines:
- YouTube: 12 to 18 months before search-driven views become a steady lead source. Shorts can pop sooner but convert less reliably.
- Pinterest: 3 to 6 months for pins to start ranking on board and search queries. Older pins keep working for years.

Tools used end to end:
- Canva: Pinterest pin templates, YouTube thumbnails, B-roll title cards.
- CapCut: Shorts edits, long-form rough cut, captions.
- TubeBuddy: keyword research, tag optimization, A/B thumbnail tests.
- Tailwind: Pinterest scheduling, board rotation, Smart Loop on evergreen pins.

## 3. Cross-Posting Workflow

One MNKB blog post powers one YouTube long-form, three YouTube Shorts, and ten Pinterest pins. Goal: every piece of effort gets reused at least 14 times.

### The 1 to 14 Rule

```
1 MNKB blog post
  -> 1 YouTube long-form (8 to 14 min)
  -> 3 YouTube Shorts (cut from the long-form)
  -> 10 Pinterest pins (different angles, all back to the blog)
```

### Weekly Schedule Template

| Day | Task | Tool | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Pick the week's blog topic. Outline long-form video. Pull keywords. | TubeBuddy | Topic brief, KW list, 5-bullet outline |
| Tuesday | Shoot long-form video. Capture B-roll. | Camera, phone | Raw footage |
| Wednesday | Edit long-form. Pull three Shorts moments. Write blog draft. | CapCut | Long-form rough cut, Shorts rough cuts, blog draft v1 |
| Thursday | Publish blog post on MNKB. Finalize long-form. Build YouTube thumbnail. | Canva, TubeBuddy | Live blog URL, finished video file, thumbnail PNG |
| Friday | Upload long-form to YouTube. Schedule three Shorts (Sat, Sun, Mon). Design 10 Pinterest pins from blog photos. | Canva, Tailwind | YouTube video live, Shorts queued, 10 pin PNGs |
| Saturday | Schedule 10 pins across boards via Tailwind (staggered 7 to 14 days). Review last week's analytics. | Tailwind, TubeBuddy | Pin queue loaded, KPI snapshot |
| Sunday | Light day. Reply to YouTube comments. Re-share top pin to relevant group board. | Browser | Engagement maintained |

### Repurposing Rules

- Never publish all 10 pins on day one. Stagger across 7 to 14 days via Tailwind. Pinterest rewards consistency.
- Each Short ends with the same line: "Full breakdown linked in the description." That description points to the MNKB blog post.
- Long-form YouTube description leads with the first 150 chars of the matching blog post intro. Then list timestamps. Then the MNKB blog URL on its own line.
- Every pin description leads with the exact target search query, then context, then a soft CTA to the blog URL.
- Every Pinterest board includes the city or style keyword in the name (Tudor Kitchens, Lake Home Kitchens, Minneapolis Bathrooms).

### Tool Stack Summary

| Tool | Job | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Canva | Pin templates, YouTube thumbnails | Brand-consistent visuals at speed |
| CapCut | Shorts and long-form edits, auto captions | Free, fast, captions improve retention |
| TubeBuddy | YouTube KW research, tag optimization, thumbnail A/B | Compounds search visibility over 12 to 18 months |
| Tailwind | Pinterest scheduling, board rotation, Smart Loop | Spreads pins, surfaces best performers |

## 4. Realistic Timeline Expectations

### YouTube: 12 to 18 Months

- Months 1 to 3: Build the bench. 12 to 16 videos live. Subscribers in the low hundreds. Almost no search traffic yet.
- Months 4 to 6: First videos start ranking on long-tail queries. Shorts may pop. Subscribers building.
- Months 7 to 12: Compounding kicks in. Older videos surface in YouTube search and Google. First inbound consult requests cite a video.
- Months 13 to 18: Steady lead source. The top 5 videos drive most of the search traffic. New uploads accelerate faster than month-one uploads did.

### Pinterest: 3 to 6 Months

- Months 1 to 2: Pin daily via Tailwind. Pins index. Saves slow. Click-through low.
- Months 3 to 4: Top pins start ranking on board and search queries. Saves and clicks step up. Blog traffic noticeable.
- Months 5 to 6: Pinterest becomes a real referrer to MNKB. Best pins keep working for years. Add fresh pins to old high-performers monthly.

### What Slows Both Down

- Inconsistent posting. Both platforms reward weekly cadence.
- Thin descriptions. Every pin and video needs the target search query in the first line.
- No internal link back to MNKB. The whole point is lead capture on the blog, not platform follows.
